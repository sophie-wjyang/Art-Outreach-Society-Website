Art Outreach Society

About us

We are a student-run non-profit organization based in North York, Toronto. Our mission is to use art as a tool to give back to the community by cultivating social and environmental change through the arts. AOS is centred around people and creativity; we aim to provide mentorship, share ideas, create connections, and empower young artists.


Our projects

Drawing From a Distance

A month-long social media art challenge to encourage students to continue being creative and making art during quarantine.

Guest speaker: Shelley Kavanagh

Shelley talked to us about her work with young people challenged by emotional disorders, and the power of art therapy.

Bob Ross Painting Workshop

Our January Art Therapy Workshop was a Bob Ross inspired acrylic workshop, in which we created beautiful landscape paintings.

Homeless Care Kits

We created DIY care packages consisting of student-sewn pillows from donated clothing, snacks, and hygiene products, which were then distributed to the homeless in downtown Toronto.

DIY ornaments

For our December Art Therapy Workshop: our very own DIY christmas ornaments — complete with a red glitter coat and Santa belt buckles.

Christmas Origami for Sunnybrook (ft. Origami Club)

Partnering with the MGCI Origami Club, we made over 100 origami ornaments that were donated to the Sunnybrook Veteran Hospital.

Postcard doodling

Therapy: $120/session. Coloring, music, and sharing our stories at AOS: 100% free.

Starbucks Cup Art For Legion

Door-to-door marketing of our hand-painted Starbucks Cups filled with goodies, all the profits of which were donated to the Royal Canadian Legion.

Expressive Art Workshop

Translating beautiful melodies and upbeat percussion into vibrant, colorful brush strokes.

Thrift Flip
(ft. Fashion Club)

Students brought in their old clothes for a complete creative makeover.

Thank You
Cards for Underappreciated Staff

By recognizing MGCI's janitors, administrative staff, and special needs workers with hand-drawn cards, we hoped to show our appreciation for everyone in our school community.

Mental Health Awareness Posters

In honour of mental health awareness month, we created dozens of posters with encouraging quotes and drawings, which were hung up all over the school.
